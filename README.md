# Graduation-Project-DEPI
 HR Performance Analysis Dashboard
A comprehensive Human Resources analytics dashboard built with Power BI, providing deep insights into workforce performance, attrition, compensation, and retention risk.

Overview
This project analyzes HR data for a company of 10,000 employees across 9 departments, covering the period from 2018 to 2024.
The dashboard answers critical questions:

Why are employees leaving?
Who is at the highest risk of leaving?
Are we paying competitively?
How is performance distributed across departments?

Dashboard Pages
1. 🏢 Executive Overview
High-level summary of workforce health including headcount, attrition trend, gender distribution, age bands, and salaries by department.
2. 💰 Compensation Overview
Analyzes total salary spend, compensation vs. market benchmarks, acquisition costs by sourcing channel, and salary distribution by education level.
3. 📈 Performance Analysis
Tracks KPI Achievement, Skill Acquisition, Quality of Work, and Pulse Score — broken down by department and potential level.
4. 🚪 Attrition Analysis
Deep dives into attrition trends by year, exit reasons, market salary position, and age band — with filters for gender, department, and year.
5. ⚠️ Retention Risk
Identifies high-risk employees, focusing on those who are both High Potential and at High Risk of leaving — the most critical segment.

🌟 8 Critical Employees: High Potential + High Risk
These 8 employees represent the highest priority for retention — losing them means losing top talent and institutional knowledge.
😶 Quiet Quitting Signal
Employees who left due to Low Engagement had a KPI of only 60.54% — the lowest among voluntary leavers, indicating disengagement long before departure.
✅ Best Performers Are Leaving Voluntarily
Employees who left for Better Offers had KPI = 75.18% — above company average — meaning competitors are poaching top talent.
